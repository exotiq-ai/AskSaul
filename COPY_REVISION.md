# AskSaul.ai — Copy & Content Revision Prompt
## Post-Build Refinement Pass

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** April 2, 2026
**Owner:** Gregory Ringler
**Agent:** Saul (OpenClaw)
**Context:** The site is built and functional. This prompt addresses copy, content framing, and structural improvements identified during review. These changes do not require new pages or features. They require rewriting, reorganizing, and adding content to existing sections.

---

## MISSION

Shift the site's center of gravity from "meet the builder" to "here's what gets fixed for your business." The site currently reads too much like a personal portfolio. It needs to read like a business solving business problems, with Gregory's story as a trust signal rather than the lead story.

Every change in this prompt serves one goal: make a first-time visitor understand within 10 seconds what AskSaul does, within 30 seconds why it matters to their specific business, and within 60 seconds what their next step is.

---

## RULES FOR THIS REVISION

- Gregory is always Gregory. Never "Greg."
- No em dashes. Use commas, periods, or restructure.
- No purple anywhere in the design.
- No fabricated testimonials, metrics, or claims.
- Use "we" on service pages and the homepage. Use "I" only on the about page.
- Every section must end with a clear path to the proposal builder or contact.
- Write for the business owner who is skeptical but curious, not for developers or AI enthusiasts.
- When describing AI capabilities, use plain language and specific daily actions. Never say "leverage," "empower," "synergy," or "cutting-edge."

## 1. HOMEPAGE REVISIONS

### 1.1 Hero Section — Rewrite

**Current problem:** The subheadline leads with "Gregory Ringler builds..." which makes this a portfolio, not a service business. The stats bar uses developer language ("commits") that means nothing to a business owner.

**New hero copy:**

Headline (keep or refine the existing one):
> Your competitors are automating. You are still doing it manually.

Subheadline (REPLACE the current one):
> Done-for-you AI assistants, high-converting websites, and marketing automation for businesses that are done wasting time on tools that don't talk to each other.

Remove "Gregory Ringler" from the hero entirely. His name is in the nav, the footer, and the about page. The hero is about the visitor, not the builder.

CTAs remain: "Build Your Proposal" (primary) and "Talk to Saul" (secondary).

**Stats bar — REPLACE with business-outcome language:**

Remove: "1,120+ commits shipped / 5 production platforms / Denver, CO"

Replace with:
> "5 live platforms built and running" / "Weeks to launch, not months" / "Your data, your infrastructure" / "Denver, CO"

These communicate speed, ownership, and locality without requiring the visitor to know what a commit is.

### 1.2 Problem Section — Refine First Pain Point

**Current:** "You have tried the chatbots. You have watched the AI demos. None of it actually works for your specific business."

**Problem:** Assumes the visitor has already tried AI tools. Many of your best prospects haven't tried anything yet. They're just drowning in manual work.

**Replace with:** "You keep hearing about AI but nothing you have seen actually fits how your business works. The generic tools feel like toys. The enterprise solutions cost more than your rent."

Keep the other two pain points as-is. They're strong.

### 1.3 Service Lanes — Add Industry Signal

After the three service lane cards, add a small section:

**Headline:** "Built for businesses like yours"

**Content:** A single row of industry tags/badges, lightly styled:
> Service Businesses / Real Estate / Legal & Consulting / HVAC & Home Services / Marketing Agencies / Med Spas & Dental / E-Commerce / Restaurants & Hospitality / Tech Startups

No descriptions needed here. Just the visual signal that says "we work with people in your industry." This creates the identification moment.

### 1.4 NEW SECTION — "What Saul Actually Does" (Add after Service Lanes, before Portfolio)

This is the single highest-impact addition to the homepage. It bridges the gap between "I'd get an AI assistant" and "oh, THAT'S what it would do for me."

**Section headline:** "What Saul does on a Tuesday morning"

**Layout:** 6 cards in a 2x3 or 3x2 grid (responsive to single column on mobile). Each card has an icon, a headline, and 2 sentences max.

**Card 1: Answers your customers while you sleep**
Saul responds to WhatsApp and Telegram messages 24/7 with answers trained on your business. No more missed leads at 11pm.

**Card 2: Drafts follow-ups from your meeting notes**
Drop your notes into the chat. Saul writes the follow-up emails, action items, and client summaries. You review and send.

**Card 3: Handles the questions your team answers 50 times a week**
Pricing, hours, availability, "do you serve my area?" Saul handles the FAQ so your team handles the work that matters.

**Card 4: Researches leads before your sales calls**
Tell Saul who you are meeting with. Get a brief on their company, recent news, and talking points before you dial.

**Card 5: Manages your inbox and flags what matters**
Saul triages your messages, surfaces urgent items, and drafts responses to the routine ones. You decide what gets sent.

**Card 6: Runs your back-office tasks on autopilot**
Data entry, CRM updates, invoice reminders, appointment confirmations. The stuff that eats 2 hours a day and produces zero revenue.

**After the cards, one line:**
> "These are real workflows running on AskSaul deployments today. Not demos. Not mockups."

**CTA:** "See how it works for your industry" linking to /ai-automation

### 1.5 Meet Saul Section — Reframe Lead

**Current problem:** The section leads with technical architecture ("runs on your own server, responds in apps your team already uses, hardened against security issues"). This is important but it's not what hooks a non-technical buyer first.

**Restructure the section intro to lead with experience, then architecture:**

Replace the current paragraph with:
> Saul is your AI assistant, set up for you, tuned to your business, and supported after launch. You talk to him in the apps your team already uses. He remembers your context, takes real actions, and gets better over time. Unlike SaaS chatbots, Saul runs on your own infrastructure, which means your data stays yours and there are no monthly seat fees.

The three feature badges below can stay but reorder them:
1. "Set up for you" (with subtext: "Configured, tested, and handed off ready to work")
2. "Security hardened" (with subtext: "Zero critical findings before every handoff")
3. "Lives in your apps" (with subtext: "Telegram, WhatsApp, Discord, or Slack")

### 1.6 Mock Chat Demo — Strengthen

**Current interaction:** Visitor asks about pricing, Saul answers with pricing. This only shows Saul as a price lookup tool.

**Replace with an interaction that shows capability:**

```
Visitor: I have a sales call with Meridian Properties in 20 minutes. What should I know?

Saul: Meridian Properties is a Denver-based commercial real estate firm. 
      They manage 12 properties across the metro area. Their CEO, Lisa Chen, 
      was quoted last month about expanding into mixed-use developments. 
      They are currently hiring a marketing director, which suggests 
      growth mode. Want me to draft talking points for the call?

[Quick replies: "Yes, draft talking points" / "Pull up their website"]
```

This demo shows Saul doing real work: research, synthesis, and proactive follow-up. It's the "oh, it can do THAT?" moment.

### 1.7 Portfolio Section — Clean Up

**Remove the two "Screenshot coming soon" placeholder cards.** Show only DriveExotiq.com and Exotiq.ai on the homepage. Two strong projects with real screenshots are better than four with two blanks. The full portfolio lives on /portfolio for visitors who want more.

**Change the section headline:**

Remove: "Built from scratch. Shipped to production."
Remove: "Every project below is live, maintained, and built by Gregory alone. No outsourcing. No templates. No shortcuts."

Replace headline with: "Recent work"
Replace sub with: "Live projects. Real businesses. Full builds."

The "solo founder" story belongs on the about page. On the homepage, the work should speak for itself.

### 1.8 Final CTA — Keep As-Is

"Ready to stop duct-taping your tech together?" is strong. Keep it.

## 2. AI & AUTOMATION PAGE REVISIONS

- Drafts listing descriptions from property details and photos
- Sends automated follow-ups to leads who went quiet

- Coordinates showing schedules across your team

**Legal / Consulting**
- Summarizes documents and case materials
- Drafts client memos from meeting notes
- Manages billable hour tracking reminders
- Researches precedents and regulatory updates
- Handles intake form processing for new clients

**HVAC / Home Services**
- Answers "do you serve my area?" and scheduling questions 24/7
- Sends appointment reminders and follow-ups automatically
- Drafts review request messages after completed jobs
- Routes emergency service requests to on-call staff
- Manages seasonal promotion outreach

**Marketing Agencies**
- Generates content briefs and social media drafts
- Monitors client campaign performance and flags issues
- Drafts client reporting summaries
- Manages project timelines and task reminders
- Handles internal team coordination across Slack channels

**Med Spas / Dental / Healthcare**
- Handles appointment booking and rescheduling via text
- Sends treatment follow-up care instructions
- Manages new patient intake questionnaires
- Responds to "do you accept my insurance?" and FAQ questions
- Coordinates referral thank-you sequences

**E-Commerce / Retail**
- Answers product questions and checks availability
- Sends abandoned cart recovery messages
- Handles order status inquiries
- Drafts product descriptions from specifications
- Manages return and exchange communication

**Restaurants / Hospitality**
- Handles reservation inquiries and confirmations
- Answers menu and dietary restriction questions
- Manages event and catering inquiry responses
- Sends post-visit review requests
- Coordinates staff scheduling reminders

**Tech Startups / Dev Teams**
- Triages GitHub issues and assigns priority
- Reviews pull requests and flags concerns
- Monitors CI/CD pipelines and alerts on failures
- Drafts technical documentation from code changes
- Manages sprint standup summaries

### 2.2 Tier Selection Simplifier — Add Above Pricing Cards

Add a quick qualifier section above the four pricing tier cards:

**Headline:** "Not sure which tier? Start here."

**Layout:** Three simple lines with an arrow or visual indicator pointing to the recommended tier:

> **Just you?** → Starter ($500)
> **Small team, 2-10 people?** → Team ($1,000)  
> **Customers will talk to the bot?** → Pro ($2,500)
> **Developer or technical team?** → Dev ($1,200)

This reduces cognitive load before the visitor even looks at the detailed tier cards. Most people will know immediately which line applies to them.

### 2.3 "Meet Saul" / Demo Section — Add to AI Page

If the homepage gets the stronger mock chat demo (Section 1.6), consider adding a second demo scenario on the AI automation page that shows a different use case. For example, an internal team scenario:

```
      It's sitting in your drafts folder. Want me to send it now or flag 
      it for your review first?

[Quick replies: "Flag for review" / "Send it now"]
```

This shows Saul operating in a team context with real workflow awareness.

### Tone Calibration
The target reader is a business owner between 30 and 55 who:
- Makes $100K-$500K in revenue
- Has 1-20 employees
- Knows they need better technology but doesn't know where to start
- Has been burned by at least one overpromising SaaS tool or freelancer
- Is skeptical of AI hype but curious about practical applications
- Will spend $2K-$15K on the right solution if they trust the provider

Write every line for that person. Not for VCs. Not for developers. Not for AI Twitter. For the person sitting at their desk at 9pm on a Wednesday wondering why they're still doing this manually.
