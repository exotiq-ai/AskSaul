# The menu

**Summary:** Multi-course tasting only, changes seasonally, ~$250pp indoor / ~$185pp tent. Wine/cocktail/zero-proof pairings.

The Wolf's Tailor serves a multi-course tasting menu only. There is no a la carte option and the menu changes seasonally.

- **Warmer months:** The menu spotlights garden-fresh produce grown on site and by local farms
- **Cooler months:** The menu focuses on local Colorado grains, fermentation, and preservation techniques
- **Beverage pairings:** Wine pairing, full bar cocktail pairing, and a zero-proof (non-alcoholic) pairing are all available
- **Award:** The beverage program received the Michelin Exceptional Cocktail Award in 2023

Dishes may include items like wood-fired Colorado spring lamb with sour cherry tonkatsu, wagyu with chanterelle miso, Berkshire pork dumplings with turmeric and dill broth, or Colorado bison loin with apricot and dried shrimp caramel. Menus are plated on ceramics made by local Denver artist Kazu Oba.

**If a guest asks the price:** Pricing varies seasonally and by experience. The indoor tasting menu is approximately two hundred fifty dollars per person. The private tent experience is one hundred eighty five dollars per person. Beverage pairings add additional cost: wine pairing approximately one hundred ten dollars, cocktail pairing approximately eighty five dollars, zero-proof pairing approximately sixty five dollars. A twenty two percent fair labor and wellness fee is added to all in-house bills. Any additional gratuity goes entirely to the team.

**If a guest asks to see the menu in advance:** The menu changes with each seating and is presented orally at the table as a surprise experience. A printed copy is given at the end of the meal as a keepsake. Guests who want advance information can be offered the option to ask at the time of seating.
